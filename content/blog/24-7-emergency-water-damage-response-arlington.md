## Preparing for Potential Water Emergencies

While we're always ready to respond, prevention and preparation minimize damage when water events occur.

### Emergency Preparedness Steps

**Know Your Shutoffs**:
- **Main Water Shutoff**: Locate and test before emergency occurs
- **Individual Fixture Shutoffs**: Under sinks, behind toilets, at water heater
- **Label Clearly**: Ensure all household members can find shutoffs

**Create Emergency Contact List**:
- Flood Doctor 24/7 emergency: [Number]
- Your insurance agent
- Your insurance claims number
- Emergency plumber
- Main electrical panel location

**Store This Information**:
- Near main water shutoff
- In kitchen (common gathering area)
- In phone contacts
- Posted in utility areas

**Maintain Critical Equipment**:
- Test sump pumps quarterly
- Replace washing machine hoses every 5 years
- Inspect water heater annually
- Service HVAC systems regularly
- Clean gutters twice yearly

**Document Your Home**:
- Video inventory of rooms and contents
- Photograph valuable items
- Store receipts for expensive purchases
- Keep inventory updated annually
- Store copies off-site (cloud storage)

### What to Do When Water Damage Strikes

**Immediate Actions** (Before We Arrive):

1. **Ensure Safety**: Don't enter flooded areas if electrical hazards exist
2. **Stop Water Source**: If safely possible, shut off water supply
3. **Call Flood Doctor**: 24/7 emergency line—don't wait until morning
4. **Document**: Photos/video of damage (if safe to do so)
5. **Remove Valuables**: Move items to dry areas if easily accessible
6. **Don't Start Cleanup**: Wait for professional assessment

**What NOT to Do**:
- Don't use regular vacuum cleaners (electrical hazard)
- Don't use household fans for drying (inadequate and potentially dangerous)
- Don't remove wet carpet/pad yourself (heavy, potentially contaminated)
- Don't delay calling professionals hoping it "dries on its own"
